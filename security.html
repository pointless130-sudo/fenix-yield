Stay safe

Security Best Practices

Fenix Yield is non-custodial — your funds never leave your Safe wallet. But that means you're the last line of defense. Follow these practices every time you approve a transaction.

✅ The golden rule: Fenix Yield will NEVER ask you to send ETH to an unknown address, approve unlimited token spend, or change your Safe owners. If you see any of these — reject immediately.
  • 1
    Always review the transaction destination in Safe Mobile Before tapping approve, check the "Interacted with" address. It should be one of: Lido (0xae7ab96...), Curve stETH pool (0xDC24316...), Aave Gateway (0xEFFC18...), or Convex Booster (0xF403C1...). If it's any other address — reject.
  • 2
    Check the ETH value being sent Lido, Curve, and Aave transactions will send your stake amount in ETH. Convex deposit and harvest transactions send zero ETH. If a transaction sends an unexpected amount — reject.
  • 3
    Verify the function being called Tap "Transaction details" in Safe Mobile to expand the call. You should see: submit() for Lido, add_liquidity() for Curve, depositETH() for Aave, or deposit()/getReward() for Convex. Anything else is suspicious.
  • 4
    Approve one transaction at a time The bot proposes one transaction per cycle and waits. If your Safe queue has multiple pending transactions you didn't initiate, pause the bot with /pause and investigate before approving anything.
  • 5
    Keep your MetaMask seed phrase offline Your MetaMask wallet is a signer on your Safe. If your seed phrase is compromised, an attacker could approve malicious transactions. Write it on paper, store it securely offline — never digitally.
  • 6
    Never approve from a public or shared device Only approve Safe Mobile transactions from a personal device you control. Public WiFi and shared computers can expose your wallet to interception.

Stay alert

Recognising Malicious Messages

The bot communicates only through Telegram. Here's what legitimate messages look like — and red flags to watch for.

🚨 Fenix Yield will NEVER: Ask for your seed phrase or private key. Ask you to send ETH directly to a wallet address. Tell you to approve a transaction "urgently" or you'll lose funds. Send you links to sites other than app.safe.global or fenix-yield.com.
🚫 Red Flag — Fake bot messages
Scammers create fake Telegram bots with similar names. Always verify you're talking to the correct bot by checking its username matches what the admin gave you at signup. If a "Fenix Yield" bot contacts you that you didn't add yourself — block it.
🚫 Red Flag — Approving unknown contracts
If Safe Mobile shows a transaction to an address not listed in the "What to expect" section above, reject it. Legitimate bot transactions only go to the 5 protocol contracts listed. The bot will never propose a transaction to an arbitrary wallet address.
🚫 Red Flag — Unlimited token approvals
Token approve transactions (for Convex) should show a specific amount matching your LP token balance — not "unlimited" or a huge number like 115792089237... (type(uint256).max). If you see an unlimited approval to any address other than the Convex Booster — reject.
🚫 Red Flag — Owner or threshold changes
The bot will never propose a transaction that adds or removes Safe owners, or changes the signing threshold. If you see "addOwner", "removeOwner", or "changeThreshold" in a pending transaction — reject immediately and contact support.
✅ What legitimate bot messages look like
Messages from the bot include: APY updates, stake proposals with protocol name and ETH amount, harvest notifications with CRV/CVX amounts, risk alerts with specific protocol names, and weekly reports. All messages are factual and never create urgency or ask for credentials.
⚠️ If you're ever unsure: Use /pause in Telegram to stop the bot, then check your Safe queue at app.safe.global. Reject anything you didn't expect. Better to miss a yield cycle than approve a malicious transaction.

Under the hood

How Autopilot Works — Zodiac Roles Module

Autopilot uses the Zodiac Roles Modifier — an audited, open-source smart contract by Gnosis Guild that enforces strict execution boundaries on the bot.

🔬 The Zodiac Roles Modifier is open-source and audited. You can verify its source code at github.com/gnosisguild/zodiac-modifier-roles. The module you deploy is your own contract — Fenix Yield has no admin access to it.
  • 1
    You deploy the Roles Modifier During Autopilot setup, you deploy your own Zodiac Roles Modifier contract via the Safe app. This contract is owned by your Safe — only you can change its permissions.
  • 2
    Bot is granted a role with strict limits The setup flow executes a series of permission transactions that assign the Fenix Yield role to the bot's proposer wallet. The role only permits the specific yield protocol functions listed above — nothing else.
  • 3
    Bot calls execTransactionWithRole When executing a yield transaction, the bot calls execTransactionWithRole() on your Roles Modifier. The Roles Modifier checks: is the caller assigned this role? Is the target address whitelisted? Is the function whitelisted? Only if all checks pass does it forward the transaction to your Safe.
  • 4
    Any deviation is rejected on-chain If the bot attempted to send ETH to an unrecognized address, call an unwhitelisted function, or exceed its permissions in any way — the Roles Modifier would revert the transaction at the smart contract level, before any funds move.
  • 5
    You can disable anytime Go to app.safe.global → Settings → Modules → find the Roles Modifier → Disable. Or send /whitelist disable to the bot. The module is instantly disabled and the bot loses all execution capability.
🔐 Security guarantee: Even if Fenix Yield's servers were completely compromised, an attacker could not steal your funds. The Roles Modifier smart contract enforces all boundaries on-chain, independent of any off-chain code.
⚠️ Known setup requirement: When deploying the Zodiac Roles Modifier, use the original Zodiac app in Safe (labeled "The expansion pack for DAOs"), not the "Zodiac Roles" app which lacks a deploy button. Also ensure your Safe transaction queue is empty before deploying — pending transactions block deployment due to nonce ordering.
